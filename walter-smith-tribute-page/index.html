Career And Influence

LEGENDARY MANAGER WALTER SMITH:

There are a small number of people who have made such an incredible contribution to Scottish football that they become known by one name. ‘Fergie’, ‘King Kenny’ and standing alongside them is ‘Walter’. During two spells as manager of Rangers, Walter won 21 domestic honours and led the Ibrox side to the final of the UEFA Cup in 2008. Smith was a close friend of Sir Alex Ferguson, spending time as his assistant at Manchester United after managing Everton. Walter is warmly remembered for his spell in charge of the Scotland National team where he restored pride in the team after a troubled spell. Walter is everything a Rangers manager should be and modern Rangers fans have grown up basking in the success he brought the club. it's unlikely there will ever be another manager and person at Ibrox who commands respect and admiration like Walter Smith.


Here is a timeline of his career and achievements.

  • 1948: Smith is born in Lanark and grows up a Rangers fan before starting his football career with junior club Ashfield.
  • 1966: The then-18-year-old defender signs for Dundee United under boss Jerry Kerr.
  • 1971: Legendary manager Jim McLean joins United and becomes Smith's mentor as his playing time increases.
    • 1975: Smith leaves Tannadice to join Dumbarton where he stays for two years, before returning to the City of Discovery to cut his teeth in coaching as injury forced him to retire from playing aged 29. During his time at United, Smith would work with the reserve players he played alongside and train young talent - he was also the manager of Scotland's U18 side.
  • 1982: His work winning the U18 Euros in 1982 would earn Smith a promotion to Scotland U21 boss as he combined that post with his position as United assistant - helping McLean guide the team to the 1983 league title and two League Cups in 1979 and 1980.
  • 1986: Smith would assist Sir Alex Ferguson at the World Cup in Mexico following the sudden death of Scotland boss Jock Stein and become a director at Tannadice. However, those posts wouldn't last long as he joined Rangers to assist player-boss Graeme Souness.
  • 1991: He would take over from Souness, who departed for Liverpool as Ibrox gaffer, aged 43, and it was here that smiths career truly took off, leading them to seven titles as boss as well as three Scottish and three League Cups in his first spell at rangers, also taking them within 90 mins up a champions league final, securing 2-1 wins home and away v Leeds Utd in the process.
  • 1997: Smith is awarded his OBE for his services to football.
  • 1998: Smith would depart Govan for English Premier League outfit Everton where he would remain for four years in a tough period for his career, but would leave a lasting impression on young players at the club such as Wayne Rooney.
  • 2004: Following his sacking at Goodison Park in 2002, Smith would return to football as Sir Alex Ferguson's assistant at Man Utd in 2004 - helping them win the FA Cup. It was a short stint, though, as Scotland came calling and Smith accepted. He would lay the foundations for Alex McLeish to take the national team on after a turbulent Berti Vogts era. A 1-0 win over France at Hampden was his most famous result.
  • 2007: With his beloved Rangers struggling after the paul Le Guen era, Walter returned to Ibrox for another Trophy laden stint, where he won a further three league titles, three League Cups and two Scottish Cups with Ally McCoist as his assistant. He also masterminded an historic run to the 2008 Uefa cup final in Manchester, beating teams such as Sporting Club and Fiorentina en route.
  • 2011: Walter Smith leaves Rangers, bringing to an end, one of the most successful careers of any manager in scottish history, and cementing his place as a Rangers and Scottish footballing great.
  • 26th October 2021:Walter smith sadly passes away after a short battle with cancer. He will be remembered as a great manager, and a better man, and his influence on Rangers Football Club will last forever.
